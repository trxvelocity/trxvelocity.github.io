The TRX Velocity Plugin allows quick velocity checks on your application by looking at various different factors which might not be suspicious in isolation but become suspicious when they've taken place within a certain timeframe.

For example, a user attempting to pay with one or two frozen credit cards is nothing unusual. They might have forgotten, or someone they share the account with might have blocked them. However, that same user attempting to pay with five different frozen credit cards within ten minutes is highly suspicious, as even someone in dire straits would likely stop once they realize one or two of their cards have been frozen or canceled. On the contrary, a criminal attempting to find which of their stolen credit cards work (a process called card testing) is very likely to do just that, repeatedly.

Fraud prevention measures such as velocity checks are increasingly important for businesses, with 80% of organizations reporting they experienced payment fraud attacks or attempts in 2023, an increase of 15 percentage points from 2022. Below, we'll explain the types of velocity checks, how TRX Velocity plugin works and why you should use it in your application.

Types of velocity checks

There are several types of velocity checks, each addressing different aspects of transaction patterns to detect potential fraud.

  • IP address velocity checks: These checks analyze the number of transactions originating from the same IP address within a specific time frame. A high number of transactions from the same IP address could indicate a fraudulent actor is using automated tools or bots to make multiple purchases.

  • Device ID velocity checks: These checks focus on the number of transactions made from the same device within a certain period. This can help identify cases in which a fraudulent actor is using the same device to make multiple purchases.

  • Account velocity checks: These checks monitor the number of transactions made from a specific account in a given amount of time. This can be useful for detecting account takeover fraud, where a fraudulent actor gains access to a legitimate account and makes unauthorized transactions.

  • Transaction amount velocity checks: These checks monitor the total amount of money spent in a certain time frame. An unusually high amount of spending could be a sign of fraud.

How TRX Velocity Plugin works

TRX Velocity Plugin is an in-product monitoring tool which and it's engine contains thresholds set for different aspects, such as transaction frequency, amount, IP addresses, devices used, and geographic locations. These thresholds can be customized to fit specific risk profiles for the business or industry

Once integrated, this engine continuously monitors transaction activities across several parameters to detect unusual patterns. It uses the thresholds to evaluate whether the behavior associated with a payment method is typical or potentially suspicious.

Here's a look at how these checks typically work:

  • Monitoring transactions: As transactions occur, the system continuously monitors them against these established thresholds. It analyzes factors such as the number of transactions within a certain time frame, the cumulative amount spent, or repeated use from the same IP address or device.

  • Detecting anomalies: When a transaction or a series of transactions breach the preset thresholds, the system flags them as anomalies. For instance, if aan account that typically makes three transactions a day suddenly makes 10 transactions in an hour, this unusual behavior would trigger a flag.

  • Taking action: Once an anomaly is detected, automated systems can take immediate action by blocking a transaction, requesting additional authentication, or flagging the transaction for manual review.

Why use TRX Velocity Plugin?

TRX Velocity Plugin, unlike other tools is a package that is installed within your application or product. This means that your data is contained and not shared with any third-party.

It is a proactive defense system that can be used to limit the immediate impact of fraud by:

  • Identifying high-risk transactions:The TVP engine can spot transactions that deviate from a user's normal behavior patterns.

  • Preventing automated attacks: Automated fraud attempts, such as those done with bots, often involve rapid sequences of transactions or logins from the same IP address or device. TVP can quickly detect and block these attempts by identifying the unnaturally high speed and volume of activity.

  • Triggering multifactor authentication (MFA): When TVP flags a transaction as suspicious, it can trigger MFA security measures. This step requires the user/merchant to provide extra verification (such as a code sent to their phone) to proceed with a transaction, adding a layer of security.

  • Analyzing geolocation: TVP can detect transactions initiated from physically improbable locations (if the geolocation feature is turned on) within a short time frame, which can indicate a compromised account.

  • Cost: TVP is inexpensive, compared to other tools in it's class. It also does not require monthly subscriptions, making it extremely cost effective.

  • Regulatory and privacy concerns: Most businesses must adhere to strict data privacy regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in Europe and California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). As a result of this privacy regulatory compliance, using other tools can be complicated and costly. TVP does not share your data or require you to share your data with any third-party. It is an in-platform plugin that does it's job within your product.

TRX Velocity Plugin Limitations

TVP is a powerful fraud management tool, however, here are some common challenges our users experience:

False positives

Risk of false positives, in which legitimate transactions are flagged as suspicious. This can happen because of:

  • Overly strict rules that flag normal spikes in user activity (e.g., holiday spending, sale spending) as fraudulent.

  • Customer behavior can vary across segments, and a one-size-fits-all approach can lead to legitimate transactions being blocked.

False negatives

Though less common, false negatives in which fraudulent transactions are not detected and are allowed to proceed can also occur. This can happen because of:

  • Sophisticated fraud techniques such as paced transactions that avoid triggering velocity thresholds.

Disclaimer

TVP checks are strict and can secure transactions but may impact the user experience, depending on your application. Each flagged transaction might require additional verification steps, delaying processing time, and frequent false positives can frustrate customers, potentially driving them to more user-friendly alternatives.

Please ensure to review your application against these limitations before reaching out to us.
